## 1. MSFT LEAPS — Beaten-down quality recovery

**Risk/Reward:**

- Target: 40-60% on the option if MSFT retraces half its drawdown over 3-6 months.
- Early exit: close if MSFT breaks decisively below the recent low that set the 18% drawdown, or if thesis (recovery) invalidates with a fresh leg down in tech.

## 2. SPY Bull Put Spread — Premium harvest on the bounce
**Structure:** Sell put spread, ~30-45 DTE. Sell put ~5-6% below spot (~715), buy put ~10 points lower (~705). 10-point wide.

**Why:** VIX declining from 25→18 means you're selling elevated-but-falling premium. The 725 low just held and produced a sharp reversal. A put spread below that level lets you profit from time decay + stability + continued grind up, with fully defined risk. This is your "get paid to be neutral-to-bullish" trade.

**Size:** 2-3 spreads. Max risk per 10-wide spread = $1,000 minus credit. 3 spreads ≈ $2,500-2,800 max risk.

**Risk/Reward:**
- Max loss: width minus credit (~$2,500 on 3).
- Target: 50% of max credit, then close (don't get greedy holding to expiration).
- Early exit: if SPY breaks back below 725 with conviction, close it—that's your thesis breaking.

## 3. IWM Bull Call Spread — Best-structure momentum play
**Structure:** Bull call spread, ~45-60 DTE. Buy ATM call, sell call ~5-7% OTM. Debit spread.

**Why:** You said small caps have the strongest structure. Small caps are also rate-sensitive and tend to lead when risk appetite returns post-pullback. A debit call spread caps cost and risk while giving leveraged upside to the strongest part of the tape. This is your cleanest momentum expression.

**Size:** 3-5 spreads depending on IWM price and spread width. Target ~$2,000-3,000 total debit/risk.

**Risk/Reward:**
- Max loss: net debit paid.
- Target: 75-100% on debit if IWM continues its trend into the short strike.
- Early exit: trail it—if IWM rolls over and loses the breakout level that defined the strong structure, cut it. Don't hold a momentum trade once momentum dies.

## 5. Covered Call — Income on a core holding
**Structure:** Buy 100 shares of a stable name you'd want to own anyway (a quality dividend payer or an ETF you're comfortable holding). Sell a call ~30-45 DTE, ~3-5% OTM (~0.30 delta).

**Why:** With VIX at 18 you're still collecting decent premium. This is your lower-volatility, income-generating position that balances the four directional/leveraged plays above. After a sharp 2-day +4.4% run, selling calls into strength is sensible—you're monetizing the bounce.

**Size:** ~$8,000-12,000 for 100 shares of a ~$80-120 stock (or fewer shares of something pricier). One covered call.

**Risk/Reward:**
- Max loss: stock can drop (downside is owning the stock minus premium collected—not unlimited but real).
- Target: collect full premium + any appreciation to strike. ~1-2% monthly yield on premium alone.
- Early exit: roll the call up/out if the stock rips past your strike and you want to keep upside; or just let it get called away for the gain.
